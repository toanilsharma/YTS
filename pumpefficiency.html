The 3 Losses
Total Efficiency (η) is a product:
1. Hydraulic (ηhyd)
(Fluid friction in casing & impeller)
2. Volumetric (ηvol)
(Internal leakage / recirculation)
3. Mechanical (ηmech)
(Friction in bearings & seals)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Step 1: Output Power
"Water Horsepower" (Pw)
Pwater (kW)
(Q × H × ρ × g) / 3.6e6
Q = Flow (m³/hr)
H = Head (m)
ρ = Density (kg/m³)
g = Gravity (9.81 m/s²)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Step 2: Input Power
"Brake Horsepower" (Pb)
Pbrake (kW)
This is the actual power delivered to the pump SHAFT.
How to Find It
Pbrake = Pmotor_elec × ηmotor
(Motor's electrical input × motor's efficiency)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The Final Formula
Pump Efficiency (ηpump)
ηpump = Pwater / Pbrake
(Output Power / Input Power)
Example
Pwater = 75 kW
Pbrake = 100 kW
ηpump = 75%

The "Expert" Factor
Best Efficiency Point (BEP)
What is BEP?
The SINGLE point on the pump curve with the highest efficiency.
WARNING
Operating away from BEP drastically increases wear, vibration, and cost.

The "Real" Metric
"Wire-to-Water" Efficiency
This is your TRUE total cost.
ηtotal =
ηpump × ηmotor × ηvfd
(Accounts for ALL system losses)
