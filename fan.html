Step 2: Pressure (Ps)
"What must the fan OVERCOME?"
System Resistance
This is the SUM of all pressure drops in the system. You must calculate it.
PTotal = PStatic + PVelocity
The fan must supply BOTH, but is rated by Static Pressure.

THE #1 MISTAKE
Ignoring Air Density (ρ)
Fans move MASS, not volume.
Fan curves are rated at Standard Air Density (1.225 kg/m³ or 0.075 lb/ft³).
Your air is NOT standard!
It's hot. It's at altitude. It's flue gas. You MUST correct for it.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Density Correction
(As per AMCA 210)
Pressure Correction
Pactual = Pstd × (ρactual / ρstd)
Power Correction
BHPactual = BHPstd × (ρactual / ρstd)
Hot air is less dense. It requires LESS power but the fan produces LESS pressure.

The Fan Laws
(How Speed (N) Changes Performance)
Flow (Q) ∝ N
(Speed 2x = Flow 2x)
Pressure (P) ∝ N²
(Speed 2x = Pressure 4x)
Power (BHP) ∝ N³
(Speed 2x = Power 8x!)

The Expert Step
The Fan & System Curve
Your Duty Point
This is where your calculated System Curve (Q vs Ps) intersects the OEM's Fan Curve.
AVOID STALL / SURGE
Your duty point MUST be in the "Stable Operating Range," (to the right of the curve's peak).

Dos & Don'ts (AMCA 201)
Installation is KEY
DO: Use VFDs for control (not dampers).
DO: Install 5-10 diameters of straight duct at the INLET.
DO: Use vibration isolators.
DON'T: Block the inlet (causes swirl).
DON'T: Run in "Stall" (unstable flow).
