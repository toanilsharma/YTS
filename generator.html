The #1 Sizing Trap
kW vs. kVA
Your 1000 kW load is NOT a 1000 kVA generator!
Motors = Inductive Load
They have a Power Factor (e.g., 0.85).
Generator kVA =
Load (kW) / Power Factor (PF)
1000 kW / 0.85 PF = 1176 kVA

STEP 1
Define Application
(As per ISO 8528-1)
Standby (ESP)
Grid failure backup. Must start in <10s (NFPA 110). Not for 24/7 use.
Prime Power (PRP)
For off-grid sites or peak shaving. Can run 24/7 but with variable load.
Continuous (COP)
24/7/365 at full base load. The most robust (and expensive) rating.

STEP 2: The "Hidden" Trap
De-rating Factors
A 1000 kVA gen is only 1000 kVA at sea level & 25¬∞C.
Altitude
Thinner air = less power. De-rate ~3.5% for every 300m (1000ft) above sea level.
Temperature
Hotter air = less power. De-rate ~2% for every 5¬∞C above 25¬∞C.

STEP 3
Analyze Your Load
Running Load
Sum of all continuous loads (kW/kVA) + Diversity Factor.
Starting Load (LRA)
The REAL killer. The Locked Rotor Amps (kVA) of your largest motor.
Harmonic Load (THD)
VFDs, UPS, LED lighting. This "noise" heats up the alternator.

STEP 4: The "Starting" Trap
The Starting kVA Trap
Your 1000 kVA gen CANNOT start a 1000 kVA motor!
Voltage Dip
A motor's inrush (LRA) is 6-8x its rating. This causes a massive voltage dip.
If dip is >30%, contactors drop out and your plant trips. (NEMA MG1)
Pro Solution
Use VFDs / Soft Starters to reduce LRA, or... size the gen 3x larger.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 5: The "Harmonic" Trap
VFDs & UPS Loads
These loads export "noise" (harmonics) back to the generator.
The Problem
Harmonics (THD) create triple-currents in the alternator's neutral, causing extreme overheating.
The Pro Solution
MUST specify an **Oversized Alternator** and a **PMG/AREP Excitation** for stable voltage.

STEP 6: The "Transient" Trap
ISO 8528-5 Class
How fast must your gen respond?
G1 / G2 Class
OK for simple loads (lights, heaters). Slower response, wider dip.
G3 / G4 Class
MUST HAVE for critical loads (UPS, VFDs, Medical). Fast response, tight voltage/frequency control.

STEP 7: The "Uptime" Secret
Redundancy (N+1)
What if your *only* generator fails?
N+1 Design
For a 1000 kVA load, install 2 x 1000 kVA gensets. (Or 3 x 500 kVA).
Paralleling Gear
Requires advanced controllers to auto-sync and share the load. This is the ultimate in reliability.

STEP 8: The "Legal" Trap
Compliance IS NOT Optional
This can stop your project cold.
Emissions
Must meet local laws (e.g., EPA Tier 4 Final, Euro Stage V). Non-compliance = massive fines.
Noise
Must meet local property line limits (e.g., 70 dBA @ 7m). Requires a "Super Silent" acoustic enclosure.

STEP 9: The Payback
CapEx vs. OpEx (Fuel)
Diesel
Pro: Best load response (kVA), lower CapEx.
Con: High OpEx (fuel cost), complex fuel storage.
Natural Gas (Gas)
Pro: Low OpEx (fuel cost), cleaner emissions.
Con: Higher CapEx, weaker load response.
Payback: Gas gens cost more upfront but save thousands in fuel.

STEP 10: The "Transfer" Trap
ATS Types
How fast is your "automatic" transfer?
Open Transition (Std)
Breaks from Grid, then Makes to Gen. A 1-2 second "blink." Fine for motors.
Closed Transition
(Make-Before-Break) Syncs to grid, parallels for <100ms. NO blink. Vital for critical loads.

STEP 11: The "Grounding" Trap
3-Pole vs. 4-Pole ATS
This is a critical safety choice.
3-Pole (Std)
Switches the 3 Phases. Neutral is common. Simple. (Requires single ground point at service).
4-Pole (Switched-N)
Switches all 3 Phases + Neutral. Creates a "Separately Derived System." Required by many codes.
