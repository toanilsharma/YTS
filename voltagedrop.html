The Power Problem
Why It's Critical
Motors (Running)
> 5% Drop
Overheating, torque loss, reduced life, inefficiency. (Ref: IEC 60364-5-52)
Motors (Starting)
> 15% Drop
Motor STALLS, fails to start, trips breaker. (Ref: IEEE Std 3004.8)

The Control Problem
Signal Integrity Failure
Analog (4-20mA)
"Ghost" Readings
Insufficient voltage for transmitter. Loop fails, shows 0mA or false reading.
Digital / Fieldbus
Data Corruption
Signal degrades, packets lost, intermittent control failure.

The Standards
The Final Authority
IEC 60364-5-52
Recommends 3% (Lighting), 5% (Other). This is a *recommendation*, not a hard rule.
IEEE Std 3004.8
Recommends max 15% drop at motor terminals during START-UP.
Vendor Specs
ALWAYS follow the manufacturer's specific voltage/length limits for VFDs & Fieldbus.
