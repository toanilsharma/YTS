STEP 2: Sizing Method
"Equal Friction"
The Goal
Size ALL duct runs for the *same* friction loss per meter (e.g., 0.8 Pa/m).
The Tool
A "Ductulator" (Duct Sizing Calculator).
You input your CFM and target friction, it gives you the duct diameter.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The "Real World" Trap
Fittings = 90% of Loss!
The Trap
You only calculate friction for the straight pipe.
The Reality
Elbows, T-junctions, and FILTERS are the *real* problem.
Each fitting = "Equivalent Length" (e.g., 1 elbow = 10m of pipe).
You MUST sum these to find the "Index Run" (worst-case path).

The Trap

The Reality

The #1 Maintenance Trap
Filtration (MERV/HEPA)
The Trap
You size the fan for a CLEAN filter.
The Pro Spec
You MUST size the fan for the "Final" or DIRTY filter pressure drop.
A dirty HEPA filter can add 250 Pa (1 "wg) of loss!

The "Control Room" Trap
Noise Criteria (NC)
High Velocity = NOISE
Sizing for friction alone can result in a "jet engine" sound.
The Pro Spec
For occupied spaces, you must also limit velocity to meet a Noise Criteria (e.g., NC-35).
This often means using larger ducts than friction alone requires.

The "Expert" App
Room Pressurization
Positive Pressure
Supply > Exhaust.
Keeps dust/gas OUT. (Control Rooms, MCCs, Cleanrooms)
Negative Pressure
Exhaust > Supply.
Keeps fumes/gas IN. (Battery Rooms, Fume Hoods)

The #1 Legal Trap
Fire & Smoke (NFPA)
This is NON-NEGOTIABLE.
Ducts that cross a fire-rated wall MUST have a Fire Damper.
The "Gotcha"
These dampers create a large pressure drop!
You MUST include them in your "Index Run" calculation.
