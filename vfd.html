STEP 1: The Payback
Why Bother? (For VT Loads)
The "Cube Law" = Insane Savings
Power ∝ (Speed)³
Run a Fan/Pump at 80% Speed...
(0.8)³ = 0.512
...you use 51% Power.
That's a 49% SAVING!
Throttling (valves) just wastes this energy as heat & noise.

STEP 2: Load Type
What is the *Load Torque*?
Variable Torque (VT)
Pumps & Fans. The "Cube Law." (Power ∝ Speed³). Massive energy savings.
Constant Torque (CT)
Conveyors, Mixers, Hoists. Needs full torque at all speeds. No cube law.
Constant Power (CP)
Winders, Spindles. High torque at low speed, low torque at high speed.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 3: Control Method
Simple vs. Precision
V/Hz (Scalar)
Simple, cheap. Perfect for VT loads (Pumps/Fans) where precision isn't key.
Vector Control (SVC/FOC)
High performance. Needs 100% torque at 0 speed. MUST USE for CT loads (Hoists, Mixers).
This is a fundamental choice. Don't use V/Hz on a crane!
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 4: The "WOW" Trap
Normal vs. Heavy Duty
Size by Overload, NOT just kW!
Normal Duty (ND)
110-120% overload for 60s.
Use for VT loads (Pumps/Fans).
Heavy Duty (HD)
150-180% overload for 60s.
MUST USE for CT loads (Conveyors).

The "Duty" Secret
Why It's A "Trap"
An OEM's 110kW VFD...
110kW Normal Duty
(For a 110kW Fan)
IS THE *EXACT SAME* VFD AS
90kW Heavy Duty
(For a 90kW Conveyor)
If you put the 110kW fan VFD on a 110kW conveyor, it will trip on overload.

STEP 5: De-rating
The Non-Negotiables
Altitude & Temp
Hot & high = thin air. VFDs need air to cool. De-rate per OEM data.
Enclosure (IP / NEMA)
An IP55 (NEMA 12) VFD has no fan. It must be oversized to shed heat.
Carrier Frequency
Higher freq. = quiet motor, but MASSIVE VFD heat. De-rate heavily.

STEP 6: The "Harmonic" Trap
IEEE 519 Compliance
Standard VFDs (6-Pulse) are "dirty" and cause...
High THD (Noise)
This overheats transformers, trips breakers, and violates your utility contract.
The Fixes
Good: 5% AC Line Reactor.
Better: 12/18-Pulse VFD.
Best: AFE (Active Front End).

STEP 7: Payback (AFE)
DFE vs. AFE
DFE (Standard 6-Pulse)
Pro: Low CapEx (cheap).
Con: High Harmonics. One-way power (needs braking resistors).
AFE (Active Front End)
Pro: <5% THD (Compliant). Regenerative (saves energy).
Con: High CapEx (costly).
Payback: AFE is for cranes, hoists, & strict utility contracts.

STEP 8: The "Mega-VFD"
Above 2MW (2000kW)
Welcome to the next level...
VSI (Voltage Source)
This is 99% of VFDs. Uses IGBTs. Great for general purpose.
LCI (Load Commutated)
The "Big Gun" for 2-100MW.
Uses Thyristors. Simple, robust... but has a "wow" catch...
The LCI Catch
It requires a Synchronous Motor. It cannot run on a standard induction motor.

STEP 9: The "Braking" Trap
How Does It Stop?
Stopping a heavy load (conveyor) creates energy. Where does it go?
DC Injection
VFD shorts the motor. Harsh. OK for small loads.
Dynamic Braking (DBR)
Dumps energy into a giant resistor (a heater). This is the CT standard.
Regenerative (AFE/LCI)
Puts 100% of the energy back on the grid. Saves money.

STEP 10: The "Safety" Spec
Safe Torque Off (STO)
How do you E-Stop a VFD?
The Old Way (Bad)
Use a contactor. Slow to open, high-wear, expensive, large panel.
The New Way (STO)
A built-in, SIL-Rated safety input. Instantly disables motor torque. No contactor needed.
This is a non-negotiable for modern machine safety.

STEP 11: The "Motor" Trap
The VFD *Will* Kill Your Motor
VFDs create voltage spikes that kill motors.
Reflected Waves
With long cables (>30m), spikes reflect and can double, killing insulation.
The Pro Solution
1. MUST use Inverter-Duty Motor.
2. MUST use Shielded VFD Cable.
3. MUST use dV/dt or Sine Filter for long runs.

STEP 12: The "Noise" Trap
EMC/RFI Compliance
Your VFD will interfere with your PLCs and instrumentation.
The Problem
VFDs are "noisy" transmitters. This noise (RFI) couples into signal wires, causing bad readings.
The Pro Solution
1. MUST use Shielded VFD Cable.
2. Physically separate VFD power and instrument cables.
3. Use an RFI Filter.
