What Is It?
The Plant's "Heart"
A chiller is a machine that makes cold water (e.g., 6°C) for a process or HVAC.
It's a "heat pump": it moves heat from your process to the air/water.
Why It Matters:
This is almost ALWAYS the single largest electrical load and highest OpEx in your facility.

Managers: This IS your power bill. An efficient chiller is your #1 cost-saver.

STEP 1: The Load (Q)
"Tons of Refrigeration"
Q = ṁ × Cp × ΔT
Q = Heat Load (kW)
ṁ = Water Flow Rate (kg/s)
Cp = Specific Heat (~4.18)
ΔT = (TReturn - TSupply)
1 Ton (TR) = 3.517 kW

The #1 Secret
COP / EER
This is your "Gas Mileage"!
It measures EFFICIENCY.
COP (Coefficient of Performance)
Cooling (kW) / Input (kW)
e.g., COP of 6 = 6kW Cooling for 1kW Power
Higher COP = Lower Power Bill!

The "Payback" Choice
Air-Cooled vs. Water-Cooled
Air-Cooled (VCC)
Rejects heat to air. Simple.
Lower CapEx. MUCH Lower Efficiency.
Water-Cooled (VCC)
Rejects heat to a Cooling Tower.
Higher CapEx. MASSIVELY Higher Efficiency.
Payback for water-cooled is often < 3 years.

The #1 Process Killer
"Low Delta-T Syndrome"
The Trap
Your chiller is designed for a 5°C ΔT. But your process only gives 3°C ΔT.
The Result
The chiller can't load up. You run out of flow. You only get ~60% of your total capacity.
Your process starves, and your power bill soars.

Compressor Types
The "Engine" of the Chiller
Scroll
Small (< 100 TR). Good for staging.
Screw
Medium (50-400 TR). Great part-load.
Centrifugal
Large (300+ TR). HIGHEST efficiency. Use Magnetic Bearing (oil-free) for best OpEx.

The #1 Energy Saver
Part-Load Efficiency (IPLV)
The Reality
Your chiller runs at 100% load < 1% of the year.
The Spec: IPLV
IPLV (Integrated Part Load Value) is the *real* efficiency number to demand.
This is where VFDs on Screw/Centrifugal chillers dominate.

The #1 Plant-Design Trap
Redundancy (N+1)
The Trap
You need 500 TR. You buy one 500 TR chiller. It fails.
Your ENTIRE PLANT is down.
The Industrial Solution (N+1)
You buy 2 x 500 TR chillers. Or 3 x 250 TR chillers.
This "N+1" design means one unit can fail and the plant keeps running.

Expert System Design
Pumping Strategy
Primary-Secondary
A "decoupled" loop. Constant flow pumps for chillers, variable flow pumps for process. Robust & simple.
Variable-Primary
A single VFD pump loop. More efficient, but requires bypassing to maintain minimum flow for chillers. Complex to control.

The "Glycol" Trap
The Capacity Killer
The Trap
You need 5°C water for freeze protection, so you add 20% Glycol.
The Cost
Glycol has a lower specific heat than water. Your 100 TR chiller now only gives ~90 TR of cooling.
You MUST de-rate the chiller for glycol!

The "Future-Proof" Trap
Refrigerant (GWP)
GWP = Global Warming Potential
High-GWP refrigerants (like R-410A) are being phased out by law.
The Solution
Demand new, low-GWP options (like R-1234ze or R-513A).
This ensures your 20-year asset is compliant and serviceable.

What to tell the vendor

✓ Type (Air-Cooled / Water / Absorp.)
✓ Compressor (Screw, Centrifugal)
✓ Pumping (Pri-Sec / Variable)
✓ Efficiency: COP (Full) & IPLV (Part)
✓ Site WBT (for Water) / DBT (for Air)
✓ Glycol % (if any)
✓ Refrigerant Type (Low GWP)
