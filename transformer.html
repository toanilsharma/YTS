STEP 1
Base Load (kW)
① List all connected loads (kW)
  • Motors, Fans, Pumps
  • Lighting, HVAC, Heaters
  • Control panels, Equipment
② Sum all kW values
📊 Base Demand = Sum of all kW
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 2
Apply Factors
Demand Factor
Not all loads run at 100% simultaneously.
(Max Demand ÷ Total Load)
Diversity Factor
Not all *groups* of loads peak at the same time.
This refines your Base Load to a REALISTIC demand.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 3
Harmonic Loads?
K-Factor Rating
Are you feeding VFDs, UPS, or IT Servers?
YES? You MUST specify a K-Rated transformer (e.g., K-13).
Failure to do so = rapid overheating.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 4
Account for Losses
Transformer Losses
Heat is generated during operation.
+3% to 5%
Add this to your total load.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 5
Plan for Future Growth
Growth Allowance
Business needs increase over time.
+15% to 25%
Recommended for 5-10 years.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 6
De-Rating Factors
High Ambient Temp?
(e.g., > 40°C) You MUST de-rate kVA capacity.
High Altitude?
(e.g., > 1000m) You MUST de-rate kVA capacity (thinner air = less cooling).
As per IEC 60076 & IEEE C57.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The Final Formula
Calculated Load (kW)
+
Losses & Growth
= Total Design kW
Convert kW to kVA
kVA = (Total Design kW ÷ PF)
(PF = Power Factor, 0.8-0.95)

(Install & Testing)
✓ Grounding is NOT optional. It is a critical safety path.
✓ ALWAYS verify phasing (U,V,W) & voltage *before* energizing.
✓ Check torque on ALL terminations.
✓ Ensure ventilation/clearance matches the spec sheet.
