STEP 1: Choose Your Fighter
The Right Tool for the Job
Ball Bearings
Good for HIGH SPEED, low load. (e.g., small motors)
Roller Bearings
Good for HIGH LOAD, low speed. (e.g., gearboxes, conveyors)
Thrust Bearings
Only for PURE AXIAL load. (e.g., vertical pump shaft)

STEP 2: Basic L10
The "Ideal World" Calc
L10 = (C / P)p
(in Million Revolutions)
C = Dynamic Load Rating
(From Catalog)
P = Equivalent Dynamic Load
(Your Calculated Load)
p = The "Secret" Exponent:
3 for Ball Bearings
10/3 for Roller Bearings
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The #1 Sizing Trap!
Calculating 'P'
The Trap
'P' is NOT just the radial load. You must combine Radial (Fr) and Axial (Fa) loads.
The Formula
P = X * Fr + Y * Fa
X and Y are complex factors from the OEM catalog based on your specific load ratio.

What is 'a1'?
The Reliability Factor
This is your "Risk" factor.
L10 (90% reliability) uses a1 = 1
For Critical Apps:
L5 (95%) → a1 = 0.62
L1 (99%) → a1 = 0.21
Want 99% reliability? Your "life" is 1/5th!

What is 'aSKF'?
The "Real World" Factor
1. Lubrication (κ)
Is the oil/grease viscosity correct for the speed and temp?
2. Contamination (ηc)
How clean is the oil? Are the seals effective? (This is a HUGE factor!)
3. Fatigue Load (Pu)
Is your load below the bearing's endurance limit?

The "Lube Method" Trap
Grease vs. Oil
Grease (Default)
Simple, sealed, cheap. Good for "sealed for life" or low-duty.
Trap: Can't remove heat.
Oil (Bath / Mist)
The Pro choice. Actively COOLS the bearing and flushes contaminants.
Required for high speed & high temp.

The "Expert" Trap
Shaft & Housing Fits
The Trap
A "loose" fit (slip fit) feels easy to install.
The Reality: "Creep"
A rotating shaft MUST have a TIGHT "interference fit" on the inner ring, or the ring will slip ('creep') and fail.
Use ISO 286 (e.g., k5, M6) fits!
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The #1 Bearing Killer!
Bad Installation
THE TRAP (Brinelling)
Using a HAMMER. This creates tiny dents in the raceway. The bearing is DEAD on Day 1.
THE PRO WAY
1. Use an Induction Heater to expand the bearing.
2. Never press or impact the WRONG ring (e.g., pressing the outer ring to fit a shaft).
