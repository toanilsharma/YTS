STEP 2: Find Head (H)
This is the Total Work
It has 3 key parts...
H_total = H_static
+ H_friction
+ H_pressure
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Head: H_static
The 'Vertical Lift'
Static Head
H_static = Z_discharge - Z_suction
(Discharge Elevation - Suction Elevation)
Key Concept
This value is CONSTANT. It does not change with flow rate.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Head: H_friction
The 'Resistance'
Friction Loss
Loss from liquid rubbing against pipes, valves, elbows, & fittings.
"Wow" Factor
Friction is flow-dependent!
H_friction ∝ Q²
(Doubling flow = 4x friction)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Head: H_pressure
The 'System Pressure'
Pressure Head
H_pressure = (P_discharge - P_suction) / (ρ * g)
Example
Pumping into a 5 bar reactor requires MORE head than an open tank (0 bar).
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The Critical Check
Net Positive Suction Head
Can the pump "breathe"?
THE GOLDEN RULE
NPSH-A > NPSH-R
NPSH-A (Available)
Your system's property. (You must calculate this!)
NPSH-R (Required)
The pump's property. (From the OEM / Curve)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 3: Find Power (P)
The Final Step
1. Hydraulic Power (Water)
P_hyd = (Q × H × ρ × g)
2. Brake Horsepower (Shaft)
P_brake = P_hyd / η_pump
3. Motor Power (Electrical)
P_motor = P_brake / η_motor
η = Efficiency (from Curve)

The Pump Curve
This is your 'Map'
Find Duty Point
Where your System Curve (Q vs H) intersects the Pump Curve.
Check BEP
Your Duty Point MUST be near the Best Efficiency Point (BEP).
(Typically 80-110% of BEP flow)
