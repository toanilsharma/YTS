Power: Criterion 1
AMPACITY (Safety)
"Can it carry the load without melting?"
This is the baseline size from IEC/NEC tables.
BUT... this value is almost always "derated".
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Power: DERATING
(Why tables aren't enough)
Cables generate heat. Heat must escape.
You MUST reduce ampacity for:
1. Grouping: Cables in a tray heat each other up.
2. Ambient Temp: Hot area (e.g., 50°C) reduces capacity.
3. Burial Depth: Deeper = harder to cool.

Power: Criterion 2
VOLTAGE DROP (Efficiency)
"Will the motor have enough voltage to start?"
All cables have resistance. Long cables lose voltage (V=IR).
Rule: Typically < 3% VD for motor running, < 5% for feeders.
(Often requires a LARGER cable than ampacity!)

Power: Criterion 3
SHORT CIRCUIT (Robustness)
"Can it survive a 20,000A fault?"
The cable MUST survive the massive fault current (kA) long enough for the breaker to trip (e.g., 0.1 sec).
(This is a thermal shock calculation!)

Power: The Verdict
"The Governing Factor"
You check ALL THREE:
1. Size for Ampacity (w/ Derating)
2. Size for Voltage Drop
3. Size for Short Circuit
You MUST select the LARGEST cable size from the three calculations.

Instrument: Criterion 1
SIGNAL INTEGRITY (Noise)
"Can I read the 4-20mA signal?"
The signal is tiny (millivolts, milliamps). Nearby power cables create electrical "noise" (EMI) that can corrupt it.
The Fix: Twisting & Shielding
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Instrument: The Fix 1
Twisted Pairs
Why Twisted?
Twisting the two signal wires (+ and -) causes magnetic noise to be induced *equally* on both wires.
The receiver (DCS) reads the *difference*, so the identical noise is "common-mode rejected" and ignored.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Instrument: The Fix 2
Shielding (Screening)
What is it?
An aluminum-Mylar foil (or braid) wrapped around the pairs. It acts like a Faraday cage.
IS/OS: Individual Shield (on each pair) AND Overall Shield (on the whole cable). Best protection.
CRITICAL: Shield drain wire MUST be earthed at ONE end only (usually DCS side) to avoid ground loops.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Instrument: Criterion 2
Total Loop Resistance
"Can the loop be powered?"
A 4-20mA loop is powered by 24VDC. This must power the transmitter AND overcome all resistance in the loop (barriers, DCS card, cable).
Rule: (Rcable + Rdcs + Rbarrier) must be < ~750 Ω (depends on power supply).
(Standard 1.5mm² or 18AWG is fine for most runs < 1km).

Expert: Digital Cables
(Fieldbus / Profibus)
It's NOT about resistance.
These are high-frequency digital networks. They must be sized for:
1. Characteristic Impedance (e.g., 100 Ω)
2. Low Capacitance
(You MUST use special, certified Fieldbus cable, not standard instrument cable!)

Construction: Insulation
(PVC vs. XLPE)
This dictates Ampacity.
PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride):
Standard, flexible. Rated 70°C.
XLPE (Cross-Linked PE):
Standard for power. Rated 90°C. Can carry ~15-20% more current than same-size PVC.

Construction: Armor
(Physical Protection)
Prevents damage from impact/crush.
SWA (Steel Wire Armor):
For multicore power/instrument. Rigid.
AWA (Aluminium Wire Armor):
For SINGLE CORE power (steel is magnetic, would overheat).

Construction: Sheath
(The Outer Jacket)
The final barrier.
PVC: Standard.
LSFOH/LSZH: Low Smoke Zero Halogen. For tunnels, buildings. Doesn't release toxic gas in a fire.
HDPE/Specialty: For direct burial, oil/chemical resistance.

CRITICAL: SEGREGATION
(The #1 Install Error)
DO NOT mix cables.
Noise from power cables WILL corrupt instrument signals.
Rule: Power (LV/MV) and Instrument (I/S, NIS) cables MUST be in separate trays.
(A minimum air gap of 300mm / 1 ft is standard).
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
CRITICAL: HazArea
Intrinsic Safety (IS)
The "Blue Cable"
For explosive (Zone 0/1) areas.
The cable itself isn't special, but the *circuit* is. It's energy-limited (low V/I) so it can't create a spark.
MANDATORY: IS (blue) cables MUST be segregated from ALL other cables (Non-IS, Power).
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
CRITICAL: Glanding
(The Termination)
A cable is only as good as its gland.
The gland MUST be rated for:
1. The Cable Type: (e.g., SWA gland for SWA armor).
2. The Area: (e.g., Ex'd' Flameproof for Zone 1).
3. The IP Rating: (e.g., IP66 for water ingress).
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
CRITICAL: Dos & Don'ts
DO: Segregate Power, Non-IS, and IS cables.
DO: Check minimum bending radius (don't force it!).
DO: Earth shield drain wires at ONE end.
DON'T: Run instrument signals in a power tray.
DON'T: Use the wrong gland for the armor/area.
DON'T: Forget derating factors.
