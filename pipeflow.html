The Formula
Total Head Loss (hL)
hL = hmajor + hminor
hmajor (Pipe)
Friction loss from the pipe itself.
hminor (Fittings)
Loss from valves, elbows, tees, etc.
The #1 Trap: Forgetting hminor can be 50% of the total!

STEP 1: Find 'f'
Reynolds Number (Re)
First, find the "Flow Identity"
Re = (ρVD) / μ
ρ = Fluid Density
V = Fluid Velocity
D = Pipe Diameter
μ = Fluid Viscosity

STEP 2: The Regime
Laminar vs. Turbulent
Re < 2300 (Laminar)
Flow is smooth, predictable. The "easy" one.
f = 64 / Re
Re > 4000 (Turbulent)
Flow is chaotic. This is 99% of industrial flow. 'f' is now much harder...

STEP 3: hminor
The "Forgotten" 50%
Your pipe isn't straight.
Valves, Bends, Tees...
Each fitting has a "K-Factor" (Loss Coefficient) from a table.
ΔPminor = ΣK (ρV2 / 2)
Sum the K-Factors for ALL fittings. This is your total minor loss.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
STEP 4: The Result
Total System Loss
Total Head Loss (m)
hL = [ f(L/D) + ΣK ] (V2 / 2g)
Total Pressure (bar)
ΔP = [ f(L/D) + ΣK ] (ρV2 / 2)
This is the friction loss your pump must overcome.

The "Expert" Trap
Compressible Flow (Gases)
The #1 mistake with air or gas.
Density (ρ) is NOT constant!
As pressure drops, gas expands, velocity increases, and density changes.
The Pro Solution
1. If ΔP < 10% of Pin, using average density is OK.
2. If ΔP > 10%, you MUST use Isothermal or Adiabatic formulas.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
The "Other" Constraint
Velocity Limits (API 14E)
Erosion Limit (Liquids)
Too fast = erosion. Typical limit: 3-6 m/s (10-20 ft/s). For corrosive or sandy fluids, go lower.
Noise/Vibration (Gases)
Too fast = extreme noise & vibration. Typical limit: 60 m/s (200 ft/s).
Your pipe MUST pass both ΔP and Velocity checks.

Large Pipe (Low ΔP)
Pro: Low OpEx (small pump, low energy).
