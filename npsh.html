Step 1: NPSHA
"What Your System PROVIDES"
NPSHA Formula
Hp + Hs - Hvp - Hf
All values must be in Absolute Head (m or ft) at the pump suction.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
NPSHA Breakdown
(Hp + Hs - Hvp - Hf)
Hp = Pressure on Liquid
(Atmospheric or Tank Pressure)
Hs = Static Head
(Liquid level above pump centerline)
Hvp = Vapor Pressure
(CRITICAL! Increases with Temp)
Hf = Friction Loss
(Loss in suction pipe, valves, filter)
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Step 2: NPSHR
"What the Pump DEMANDS"
The "NPSH3" Standard
This value comes from the OEM curve. It is the point where cavitation has already begun and caused a 3% drop in head.
EXPERT TIP
NPSHR is NOT a single number. It is a CURVE that increases with flow! You must check it at your max flow.
prepared by https://designcalculators.co.in
Step 3: The Margin
(As per API 610 / ISO 13709)
Margin = NPSHA - NPSHR
Your margin must be positive, as NPSH3 is a *damage* point, not an *inception* point.
